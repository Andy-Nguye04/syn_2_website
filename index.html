Sewage Pollution within the Tijuana River Watershed

California Water Boards, Oct 11, 2023

Tijuana, Mexico.

The Tijuana River Watershed is a large binational watershed that lies across the California-Mexico border. Emerging sewage infrastructure inadequacies have created recurring sewage pollution problems on both sides of the California-Mexico border. The sewage flows degrade water quality in the Tijuana River Estuary and adjacent beach coastal waters and also pose a significant public health risk to residents and visitors along both sides of the border. There is also a lack of U.S. jurisdiction in Mexico making it hard for there to be consensus on what solutions should be reached.


Fukushima Daiichi Power Plant

World Nuclear, Aug 25, 2023

Fukushima, Japan.

On 2011 March 11, the core in Fukushima Daiichi Power plant melt down due to being struck by multiple tsunami waves. This disaster resulted in the release of radioactive materials into the environment. The cleanup and decommissioning is a long-term process which was expected to last decades. The accident has generated over a million tons of waste water that is contaminated. After years of storage and treatment of ALPS, the Japanese government and Tokyo Electric Power Company decided to release that treated water into the ocean starting last August.
